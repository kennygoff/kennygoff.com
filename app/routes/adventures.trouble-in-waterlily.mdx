# {frontmatter.title}

A beginner adventure for two to five 2nd-level characters using fifth edition

## How to run this adventure

### Beginner-friendly

_Trouble in Waterlily_ is designed to be beginner friendly and introduce new
players to fantasy role-playing. It's also designed to be simple to understand
and run as a beginner GM. It has a mix of NPC interaction, puzzles, combat, and
dungeon crawling.

This adventure is not meant to be particularly challenging or complicated. It's
meant to encourage role play and learning fifth edition mechanics. Reward
players with secrets scattered throughout this adventure for clever thinking and
good rolls.

### Pacing

This adventure is designed to be run in a single one-shot session lasting 3-4
hours. It's designed for new GMs to run in a single session to a satisfying
conclusion. The first 3 parts (**Waterlily**, **Everlong Wood**, and **Tomb
Entrance**) should be roughly half of the session. The second half should be
spent inside **The Tomb of Galanodel**.

The early parts they are meant to be flexible in content and length so you can
tune them to your players interests. If your party wants to spend all session in
town talking to NPCs you can fast forward to the Tomb once they head into the
woods. If they rush into the woods right away you can give them more to do in
the forest.

### Making it your own

This adventure is written with just enough info to give you the resources needed
to build a run a great session. Things are written simply and without long
flavor text for you to recite or memorize. This simplicity should hopefully
allow you to turn it into your own unique adventure! Add additional flavor and
descriptions when you describe the areas. Give extra personality to each NPC.
Swap out monsters and adjust puzzles.

## Background

Hundreds of years ago **Everlong Wood** was a lively forest filled with magic
and fey creatures. Led by siblings **Aramil** and **Caelynn Galanodel** the
Elven community of the forest prospered. Today a young necromancer named
**Evandor Greycastle** upends the Galanodel's resting place in search of
Aramil's spellbook and the secrets held within. He raises the dead and the
nearby village of **Waterlily** suffers the consequences.

## Setup

Our heroes are fresh faced adventurers after a successful first job. They are
looking to test themselves, prove their worth, and hopefully earn some gold. On
the road to **Roccan City** they stop in the village of **Waterlily** for some
rest before heading out on their final day of travel.

## Part I: Waterlily

### Locations

**Waterlily.** A halfling village on the outskirts of **Everlong Wood**. Small
homes and gardens lie scattered about. A town which is normally lively and
jovial has been quiet, doors closed, and people scared. A light fog looms over
the town and the stench of death is in the air.

**Town Hall.** A small building with a sign out front that reads "The Village of
Waterlily". Townmaster **Cora Tealeaf** resides inside.

**The Thirsty Root.** A two-story tavern and inn run by **Finn & Bree Reed**.
Looks like a good place to have a meal, enjoy a drink, and take a rest.

**Thorngage Home.** In the distance by one of the houses a boy (**Milo
Thorngage**) plays outside with a wooden sword.

### NPCs

- **Finn Reed**: (he/him) halfling, bartender of **The Thirsty Root**
- **Bree Reed**: (she/her) halfling, waitress of **The Thirsty Root**
- **Cora Tealeaf**: (she/her) halfling, townmaster of **Waterlily**, has red
  hair in a ponytail
- **Wellby Thorngage**: (he/him) halfling, Retired scout, has a limp and a cane
- **Milo Thorngage**: (he/him) halfling , Wellby's curious son, has a wooden
  sword
- **Pip Bucklin (Zombified)**: (he/him) halfling, recently zombified, has a
  simple bow and quiver of arrows strapped to his back

### Secrets & Rumors

- Zombies, skeletons, and ghouls have wandered into the town for the past two
  days scaring townsfolk and destroying crops
- A man named Evandor dressed in a black hooded robe came through the town a few
  nights ago
  - 2-3 players: He came alone
  - 4-5 players: He came with two companions donning black hooded robes hiding
    their faces (cultists)
- Pip Bucklin went missing yesterday while investigating the undead
  - Pip was killed and zombified
  - Pip will wander into town after 1d4 NPC interactions or if the players
    approach Milo, whichever happens first
- A lost tomb of the great wizard **Aramil Galanodel** lies a few miles north of
  **Waterlily**

### Monsters

- **[Commoner](https://open5e.com/monsters/commoner).** All residents of
  **Waterlily** are commoners.
- **[Zombie](https://open5e.com/monsters/zombie).** **Pip Bucklin** is a
  halfling zombie.

### Reward

**10gp per player.** The reward of **Cora Tealeaf** can offer if the players can
stop the undead threat.

## Part II: Everlong Wood

**Everlong Wood.** A forest filled with magic and fey folk.

A few awakened shrub rabbits cross the party's path but a dark energy persists
as they go deeper into the forest overshadowing the fey magic. Our adventurers
walk north and follow the path but realize they have been passing by the same
trees and shrubbery multiple times when they encounter **Boggle**.

### Secrets & Rumors

- A dark, deadly magic is coming from the Tomb of Galanodel
- Boggle also had Evandor Greycastle answer the riddles and he got 2 right
- Evandor's questions for Boggle
  - Where is Aramil Galanodel's tomb? _Answer: Just north in a clearing_
  - Is his spellbook in the tomb? _Answer: Yes, but Boggle does not know exactly
    where_

### Important NPCs

- **Boggle**: (he/him) fey trickster, chaotic neutral, loves riddles

### Boggle's Riddles

**Boggle** is casting an illusion to make the path loop, causing the players to
walk endlessly. Boggle offers to show them the way to **The Tomb of Galanodel**
if they answer his riddles. Boggle always lets the players pass if they play
along and answer, whether or not they answer correctly. But he will honestly
answer one of the players' questions for each correct riddle answer.

Boggle will not fight back if attacked. He will run away and send the players
towards violet fungus as revenge.

#### Riddles

- It has a golden head. It has a golden tail. It has no body. _Answer: A gold
  coin_
- Two bodies in one, the longer I stand, the faster I run. _Answer: Hourglass_
- You keep it, but it never ages. Once shared it is gone forever. _Answer:
  Secret_

#### Other ways to get past Boggle

- Dispel his illusion
- Convince him to drop the illusion
- Knock him unconscious before he can run away or have the fungus attack
- Anything your players can imagine! Reward creativity and good rolls!

## Part III: Tomb Entrance

**Tomb Entrance.** The players enter a clearing to find the entrance of the tomb
overgrown with vines and brush being guarded by two (2) **Skeletons** with
shortswords and shields bearing a half moon crest. A fairy, **Eryn**, hides
behind the tomb entrance in the brush silently motioning to the players to help!

### Secrets & Rumors

- The skeletons were raised from the dead by **Evandor Greycastle** from the
  tomb
- The half moon crest on the shields is the Galanodel family sigil
- For saving **Eryn** and defeating the **Skeletons**, she will give all the
  players +5 temp HP

### Important NPCs

- **Eryn**: (she/her) fairy, neutral good, guardian of the feywild

### Monsters

- **[Skeleton](https://open5e.com/monsters/skeleton).**

## Part IV: The Tomb of Galanodel

**Tomb of Galanodel.** the family tomb of the Galanodel wood elves, a powerful
family of wood elves the last of whom died nearly 300 years ago. They ruled over
the feywild of Everlong Wood and maintained a peaceful balance of nature and
magic.

### The Galanodel Siblings

**Aramil Galanodel** was a genius wizard known for his spellbook that had a wide
variety of spells from his travels. The more famous of the two siblings, most
today know of the Galanodel family know of Aramil.

**Caelynn** was a fierce warrior known for her strength, battle tactics, and
longsword "Moonwhisper". She became somewhat lost to history.

The two of them together led their people to prosper in the Everlong Wood until
they died nearly 300 years ago.

### Rooms

#### 1. Main Hall

A half moon is carved into the floor of the tomb surrounded by the phases of the
moon. Moonlight shining through a small skylight hole in the ceiling highlights
the moon crest on the floor of the first room revealing the words "Touched by
the light of the moon" in Elvish.

#### 2. Aramil's Library

Filled with Aramil's book collection: tomes of history, knowledge, magic and
nature. Many of the books tossed on the floor like someone was looking for
something. No spellbooks to be found. A book "The Feywild: A History of Magic
and Nature" written by Aramil Galanodel sits in a glass enclosure in the center
of the room; the glass is shattered and book knocked off it's display stand.

#### 3. Family Hall

Filled with family heirlooms, journals, and lineage documents. Also partially
trashed and searched through. A small crescent moon statue sits in the center of
the room in a glass display reading "Galanodel".

#### 4. Crypts

6 crypts of family members. Two crypts are open and empty (the skeletons are
outside). The other 4 crypts contain similar skeletons with shortswords and half
moon shields.

#### 5. Aramil's Crypt

Aramil and his wife's crypt. A book carved intothe left coffin marking Aramil's
resting place. **Evandor Greycastle** stands with a torch rooting through the
open crypt.

- Moonlight, whether natural or magical from the Moonwhisper longsword, will
  reveal a hidden room Aramil's spellbook on the back wall of his tomb room

#### 6. **Caelynn's Crypt**

Caeylnn and her wife's crypt. A sword carved into the right coffin marking
Caelynn's resting place.

- Caelynn's tomb is rigged with poison spray when opening
  - DC 15 Perception to notice the trap
  - DC 12 Sleight of Hand to disarm
  - **Poison spray.** 15ft radius. DC 15 CON saving throw. 1d8 poison damage of
    a failed save
- **Moonwhisper.** Caelynn's longsword, is inside Caelynn’s tomb buried with her

### Important NPCs

- **Evandor Greycastle**: Human necromancer (he/him), lawful evil, tall thin
  with black hair wearing black robes and a silver skull necklace
